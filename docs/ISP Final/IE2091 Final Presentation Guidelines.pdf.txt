Final Presentation Guidelines 
 
You are expected to perform the following tasks,  
1. The Final presentation is of 30 minutes duration of the final product 
2. Briefly introduce your project using marketing video (Marketing video guideline available 
in the courseweb 
3. Each member of the group will roughly get 3 minutes to present their component 
4. Same as Sprint II, demonstrate your component using an integrated project (not an 
individual piece of implementation). It would be more efficient to include a demo button 
instead of having to fill out each text box individually, which would help reduce the 
presentation time. 
5. Your presentation should include (Make this as a marketing presentation. No need to 
prepare presentation slides), 
1. Solution narrative – Use marketing video prepared 
2. Demonstration (all CRUD operations using real data) 
3. U/I Design 
4. Input Validations 
5. Unit tests performed 
6. Report generated (Recommended to have at least 1 report/dashboard summarizing 
the outputs from each member) 
7. Future work 
6. Focus on demonstrating your product in a logical order (e.g., customer, order, payment) 
7. Make sure you have enough amount of data available in your dataset prior to the 
presentation to do the demonstration effectively.  
8. You are allowed to use your laptops for the presentation. In that case, please make sure 
your laptop can be connected to the projector without any technical issues. 
9. There will be a 15-minute VIVA conducted following the presentation. 
10. VIVA will be mainly focusing on, code/database review. Therefore its, important each of 
you is aware of the code and database of your component. 
11. Make sure to update the Jira board as well 
 
You should score >= 45% for the final presentation and viva to pass the module
