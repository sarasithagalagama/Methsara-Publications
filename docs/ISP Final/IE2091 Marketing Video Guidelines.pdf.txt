1. Structure of the Video 
Grab attention 
• Start with a relatable problem or scenario 
• Use a question, short story, or striking statement 
• Example: “Ever waited 2 hours just to see a doctor?” 
Problem Statement 
• Clearly explain the real-world problem 
• Show who is affected and why it matters 
• Keep it simple and relatable (avoid technical jargon) 
Solution (Your Product) 
• Introduce the product (name + purpose) 
• Show key features visually (screenshots, UI clips, simple animation) 
• Focus on benefits, not just features 
Value Proposition 
• What makes your solution unique? 
• Why should users choose it? 
• Highlight 2–3 key advantages (e.g., faster, cheaper, smarter) 
Demo / Use Case 
• Show a quick real-life usage scenario 
• Keep it simple, don’t try to demonstrate everything 
Closing & Call to Action 
• Strong ending: slogan, message, or vision 
• Example: “Smarter healthcare, one click away.” 
2. Content Guidelines 
• Focus on storytelling, not technical explanation 
• Avoid code, architecture diagrams, or complex terms 
• Use real-life scenarios (students, patients, commuters, etc.) 
• Keep language simple and natural 
• Ensure the message is understandable to a non-technical audience

3. Visual & Audio Quality 
• Use clear visuals (screen recordings, mockups, animations) 
• Ensure readable text (no clutter, large fonts) 
• Add background music (low volume, not distracting) 
• Use voice-over or subtitles (preferably both) 
• Maintain good audio clarity (no noise) 
4. Editing Tips 
• Keep clips short and engaging 
• Use smooth transitions (avoid overuse of effects) 
• Maintain pacing don’t drag any section 
• Highlight key points with text overlays 
 5. Common Mistakes to Avoid 
• Explaining too many features 
• Reading from slides word-for-word 
• Poor audio quality 
• No clear problem statement 
• Too slow or too rushed 
• Lack of real-world relevance 
6. Evaluation Focus  
• Clarity of problem and solution 
• Creativity and storytelling 
• Demonstration of value 
• Visual and audio quality 
• Overall impact and persuasiveness 
 
A good marketing video should make someone say: 
 “I understand the problem, and I want to use this product.”
